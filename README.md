# India CPI Inflation Case Study (Excel)

## 📊 Project Overview
This project analyzes India’s Consumer Price Index (CPI) to understand inflation trends, category-wise contribution, and the impact of major economic shocks such as COVID-19 and global crude oil price fluctuations.

The analysis is performed entirely in **Microsoft Excel**, demonstrating how spreadsheet-based tools can be used to generate policy-relevant macroeconomic insights.

---

## 🎯 Objectives
- Identify category-wise contribution to CPI inflation
- Analyze long-term Year-on-Year inflation trends
- Deep dive into food inflation volatility
- Assess the impact of COVID-19 on inflation dynamics
- Study the influence of global oil price fluctuations on CPI categories

---

## 📁 Dataset Details
- Source: CPI index data published by the :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}

  - CPI-Rural (CPI-R)

## 🔍 Key Analyses & Insights

### 1. Category-wise Contribution to CPI
- Food is the largest contributor to CPI inflation
- Luxury-related categories contribute the second most
- Transportation & Communication contribute the least

### 2. Long-Term Inflation Trend (YoY)
- CPI inflation shows an upward trend post-2017
- 2020 recorded the highest inflation due to COVID-19 disruptions

### 3. Food Inflation Deep Dive (12 Months)
- Vegetables show the highest inflation volatility
- Fruits are moderately volatile
- Pulses remain relatively stable
- Vegetable prices decline sharply during Nov–Dec due to seasonality

### 4. COVID-19 Impact on Inflation
- Transportation & Communication most affected
- Fuel & Light inflation declined due to demand collapse
- Food inflation moderately affected due to government price controls
- Healthcare inflation moderately increased

### 5. Global Oil Price Impact (2021–2023)
- Transportation inflation strongly correlates with oil price movements
- Healthcare inflation least sensitive to global oil shocks

---

## 📈 Key Takeaways
- India’s inflation is structurally **food-driven**
- Inflation reacts sharply to supply-side and global shocks
- Sector-wise analysis is critical for effective policy decisions
- Excel is sufficient for robust macroeconomic and policy analysis
